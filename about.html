About Batang kali

Batang Kali is a town in the Hulu Selangor district of Selangor, Malaysia, situated about 70 kilometers north of Kuala Lumpur. Notably, it gained historical significance due to the Batang Kali massacre during the Malayan Emergency in 1948. The town is surrounded by lush landscapes, hills, and rivers, offering opportunities for nature-related activities. Additionally, the Hulu Selangor Dam, providing water to the Klang Valley, is located nearby. While Batang Kali has a historical past, it has also become a gateway to the scenic beauty of the Hulu Selangor region.
